About Me

I am currently a third-year PhD student in the School of Physics at Peking University , advised by Prof. Lijing Shao . I received my bachelor's degree in Astrophysics from the School of Physics at Peking University in 2023. My research focuses on AI-driven astrophysical data analysis and the use of large language models to accelerate scientific discovery, including related post-training researches.

Research Interests

My research interests center on statistical inference and artificial intelligence. Statistical inference refers to the process of using data analysis to infer properties of an underlying probability distribution. This principled methodology plays a central role in gravitational-wave astronomy, where statistically faithful conclusions must be drawn from diverse observational data.

I am also interested in artificial intelligence and its applications, including the use of AI to accelerate gravitational-wave data analysis and the exploration of large language models in gravitational-wave astronomy. In addition, I am interested in related post-training techniques for large language models, including how reinforcement learning can be used to enhance model reasoning capabilities.
